The Harvard natural-language processing group is a collection of researchers and students (both graduate and undergraduate) interested in computational methods for human language. This broadly includes developments in machine learning methodology, the study of artificial intelligence, and the neuroscience of language. Recently we have been following recent advances in deep learning for natural language processing.

Our group publishes research in natural-language processing and among other related areas. Recent focus areas of the group have been on character-based language modeling, deep learning for coreference resolution, and automatic summarization of sentences. We also release code to the research community.

If you are at Harvard and interested in NLP and ML, we suggest taking one of several courses we offer in the area (CS182, CS187, CS287). We also hold a weekly reading group where we discuss new research in the field and invite local speakers.
